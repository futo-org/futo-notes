# Short Rib Nihari (Serves 3–4)

A slow-cooked, fall-off-the-bone short rib curry with rich, silky gravy. Perfect for a 5-hour cook.

---

## Ingredients
- **Short ribs:** 2–2.5 lbs, bone-in  
- **Ghee or oil:** 3–4 tbsp  
- **Onions:** 2 medium, thinly sliced  
- **Garlic:** 6–8 cloves, minced  
- **Ginger:** 2-inch piece, minced  
- **Kashmiri chili powder or paprika:** 1.5 tsp (for color)  
- **Ground coriander:** 1 tbsp  
- **Ground cumin:** 1 tsp  
- **Turmeric:** ½ tsp  
- **Garam masala:** 1 tsp  
- **Optional spices for depth:** ½ tsp fennel powder + 2–3 cloves + 2 cardamom pods  
- **Flour:** 2 tbsp (for slurry to thicken)  
- **Beef stock or water:** 3–4 cups  
- **Salt:** To taste  
- **Cilantro & lemon wedges:** For garnish  

---

## Method

1. **Brown the Short Ribs (Flavor Foundation)**  
   - Heat ghee in a heavy Dutch oven.  
   - Sear the ribs until deep brown on all sides (8–10 min total).  
   - Remove and set aside.  

2. **Build the Masala Base**  
   - In the same pot, add onions and a pinch of salt. Cook slowly until **deep golden brown** (15–20 min).  
   - Add garlic + ginger and sauté 1–2 min.  

3. **Add the Spices**  
   - Add chili, coriander, cumin, turmeric, fennel (if using).  
   - Cook for 30–60 sec to toast the spices—don’t burn them.  

4. **Deglaze & Simmer**  
   - Return ribs to the pot.  
   - Add 3–4 cups beef stock (or water + 1 bouillon cube).  
   - Bring to a low simmer, then cover and **cook on very low heat for ~4 hours**, turning ribs occasionally.  
   - Meat should be almost falling off the bone.  

5. **Finish the Nihari**  
   - Mix 2 tbsp flour with ¼ cup water to make a slurry. Slowly stir into the pot to thicken the gravy.  
   - Cook another 30–45 min uncovered, skimming excess fat if needed.  
   - Stir in garam masala at the end.  

6. **Serve**  
   - Top with chopped cilantro and a squeeze of lemon.  
   - Serve with **naan or steamed basmati rice**.  

---

## Pro Tips for Maximum Flavor

1. **Cook low and slow** – Keep the pot at a gentle simmer, never a rolling boil.  
2. **Caramelize the onions deeply** – Don’t rush this step for rich flavor.  
3. **Toast the spices** – Fry briefly in ghee to bloom the flavor.  
4. **Overnight effect** – Make it a day ahead and reheat slowly for even deeper flavor.  
5. **Optional richness** – Add a spoon of bone marrow or extra ghee near the end.  
6. **Balance the finish** – A squeeze of lemon + fresh cilantro cuts through the richness.  

---

### Optional Tarka (Restaurant-Level Finish)
Heat 2 tbsp ghee with a pinch of Kashmiri chili, then pour it over the curry just before serving for an aromatic kick.
