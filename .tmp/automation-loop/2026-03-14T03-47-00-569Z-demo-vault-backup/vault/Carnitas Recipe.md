**Stovetop Carnitas Recipe**

  

**Ingredients:**

  

• 3 lbs pork shoulder (pork butt), cut into 2-3 inch chunks

• 1 tablespoon olive oil

• 1 onion, quartered

• 4 cloves garlic, smashed

• 1 teaspoon salt

• 1 teaspoon black pepper

• 1 teaspoon ground cumin

• 1 teaspoon dried oregano

• 1 teaspoon smoked paprika

• 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander

• 1/2 cup freshly squeezed orange juice (from about 1-2 oranges)

• 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice (from about 2 limes)

• 1 cup chicken broth or water

• 2 bay leaves

  

**Instructions:**

  

1. **Prepare the Pork**:

• Cut the pork shoulder into large chunks (about 2-3 inches each).

• Season the pork chunks with salt, pepper, cumin, oregano, smoked paprika, and coriander.

2. **Sear the Pork**:

• In a Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.

• Sear the pork chunks in batches until browned on all sides. Remove the pork and set aside.

3. **Cook the Aromatics**:

• In the same Dutch oven, add the quartered onion and smashed garlic cloves. Sauté for a few minutes until fragrant.

4. **Simmer the Pork**:

• Return the seared pork to the Dutch oven.

• Add the orange juice, lime juice, and chicken broth or water.

• Add the bay leaves.

• Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.

• Cover and simmer for about 1 hour, or until the pork is tender and can be easily shredded. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking.

5. **Shred and Crisp the Pork**:

• Remove the pork from the Dutch oven and shred it using two forks.

• Increase the heat to medium-high and return the shredded pork to the Dutch oven.

• Let the pork cook, stirring occasionally, until the edges are crispy and caramelized. This should take about 10-15 minutes. Be careful not to stir too frequently, allowing the pork to develop a nice crust.

6. **Serve**:

• Serve the carnitas in warm tortillas with your favorite toppings.

  

This stovetop method allows you to achieve tender and crispy carnitas without the need for an oven, making use of the Dutch oven for both simmering and crisping. Enjoy!
