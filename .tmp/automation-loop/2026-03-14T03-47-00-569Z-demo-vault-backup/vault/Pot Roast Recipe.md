### Ingredients

- **Beef**: Chuck roast or brisket (2-3 lbs)
- **Vegetables**: Carrots, celery, potatoes, onions, and parsnips (optional)
- **Aromatics**: Garlic (4-6 cloves, smashed), fresh thyme, rosemary, and bay leaves
- **Liquid**: Beef stock (2 cups), red wine (1 cup), or a mix of both
- **Seasoning**: Salt, pepper, and paprika (optional)
- **Thickening (optional)**: Flour or cornstarch to make a gravy

### Method

1. **Prepare the Beef**:
    
    - Season generously with salt and pepper.
    - Sear the beef in your Dutch oven over medium-high heat until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
2. **Sauté Aromatics**:
    
    - In the same Dutch oven, sauté the onions and garlic until softened.
    - Add carrots, celery, and any other sturdy vegetables. Cook for 3-5 minutes to develop flavor.
3. **Deglaze and Assemble**:
    
    - Pour in red wine or a bit of stock to deglaze, scraping up browned bits.
    - Return the beef to the pot, add remaining liquid, and tuck in your herbs.
4. **Cook**:
    
    - Add potatoes (and other softer vegetables) on top of the beef.
    - Cover and cook on the stove over low heat or in a 300°F oven for 3-4 hours, turning the beef occasionally.
5. **Thicken Sauce (Optional)**:
    
    - If desired, remove the beef and veggies, then thicken the sauce with a flour or cornstarch slurry.
